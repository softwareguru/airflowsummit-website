As data platforms mature, organizations often experience "Airflow Sprawl"—the rapid, organic growth of isolated Airflow instances across different teams and projects. While this empowers localized control, it creates dangerous silos that hinder visibility, increase operational risk, and erode developer productivity.
In this session, we will explore the critical challenges of managing a fragmented Airflow ecosystem and discuss strategies for regaining control. We will examine why centralizing execution history and establishing unified observability is essential for reducing Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR), mitigating hidden security risks, and transforming fragmented instances into a cohesive, reliable data service. Attendees will leave with a strategic framework for managing Airflow at scale.
