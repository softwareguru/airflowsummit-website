Meet airflowctl, the new default for API-driven remote operations. You will see how separating control from execution enhances security, enables isolation, and simplifies automation across different environments.
I will discuss the development of airflowctl, demonstrate practical examples of secure remote execution, and provide a guide for transitioning from legacy workflows. You will learn how to easily migrate towards airflowctl and leverage the flexibility of an API-driven approach.
